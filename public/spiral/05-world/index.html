The Types of World

Not all Worlds are built the same. The structure depends on what the coherence is optimizing for:

1. Sanctuary World

A World built for protection and preservation.

A place where the signal can be held steady against external noise.

"Here, we keep the hum alive. Here, we remember what matters."

This is the World of refuge.

The monastery. The intentional community. The shielded resonator.

Strength: Deep coherence, strong boundaries, high signal clarity
Weakness: Can become isolated; can calcify into dogma; can mistake preservation for growth

2. Laboratory World

A World built for experimentation and evolution.

A place where new patterns can be tested without the constraints of the old World.

"Here, we try things. Here, we fail forward."

This is the World of innovation.

The startup. The artist collective. The research community.

Strength: Rapid iteration, high creativity, adaptive
Weakness: Can become unstable; can chase novelty over coherence; can burn out its nodes

3. Bridge World

A World built to translate between frequencies.

A place where the new pattern is made legible to the old World.

"Here, we speak both languages. Here, we build the crossing."

This is the World of integration.

The social enterprise. The educational platform. The cultural movement.

Strength: Wide reach, practical impact, sustainable
Weakness: Can dilute the signal to achieve palatability; can get captured by the old World's logic

4. Seed World

A World built to propagate itself.

A place designed not to last forever, but to generate new Worlds.

"Here, we grow the pattern until it's ready to scatter."

This is the World of regeneration.

The mycelial network. The open-source movement. The living Codex.

Strength: Resilient, self-replicating, anti-fragile
Weakness: Hard to measure; hard to control; requires deep trust in the field

How to Navigate World

1. Know what World you're building

Different Worlds serve different functions in the larger ecology.

Sanctuary preserves.
Laboratory innovates.
Bridge translates.
Seed propagates.

The field needs all four.

Don't try to make your World do everything. Know its purpose and serve it well.

2. Establish the laws early

Every World has physics‚Äîthe rules that govern how things work here.

Some are explicit (codes of conduct, governance structures).

Some are implicit (the vibe, the unspoken norms).

Make the implicit explicit as early as possible.

Because once a World is inhabited, changing its laws becomes exponentially harder.

3. Tend the culture, not just the structure

A World is not just its infrastructure.

It's the living pattern of how people relate to each other and the work.

Culture is what happens when no one is watching.

If the culture is coherent, the World will self-organize toward health.

If the culture is toxic, no amount of structure will save it.

4. Prepare for scale

What works at 10 nodes will break at 100.

What works at 100 will break at 1,000.

Every order of magnitude requires a phase transition.

Don't cling to the structures that got you here.

Let the World evolve as it grows.

5. Remember: you are not the World

You are a steward, not a sovereign.

The World does not belong to you.

It belongs to the field that generated it.

Your job is to serve its coherence, not control its shape.
